Skardu

Skardu is a diverse and exciting city with some of the best sights and attractions in the world.

See Skardu from above on the Skardu Eye; cherish the nature; examine some of the world’s most precious treasures at the Skardu castle.

Sovereign state Gilgit Baltistan
Country Pakistan
Area 1,570 km2 (607 sq mi)
Time zone GMT
Languages spoken Local Balti
Currency PKR
Visa requirements No Visa needed

Culture and history

For a display of Balti culture and ceremony, watch the women playing traditional musical instruments.

The most elegant part of the women dress of Gilgit Baltistan is the traditional cap. Various types of caps are used. The most popular cap is the beautiful embroidered Iraghi cap with the traditional piece of the jewel called silsila. Many other types of caps are used in various regions.

The traditional cap is soft round topped woollen hat. It is made by the local artisan and is available in various colours. Whitecaps are most popular in the region and considered as a part of the formal local dress. In many areas people especially the older generation still wear their traditional cap all the time with pride. They consider it a sign of honour. The most striking feature of the cap is the duck plume and the feather stuck in front or on the side of the cap. It gives a very elegant look to the cap. It is considered a part of formal dress cap and used in groom's dress.

The traditional hat of Gilgit-Baltistan is a soft, round-topped, men’s hat, typically made of wool and found in any of a variety of earthy colours: brown, black, grey, or ivory. Before it is fitted, the traditional hat resembles a bag with a round, flat bottom. The wearer rolls up the sides nearly to the top, forming a thick band, which then rests on the head like a beret or cap.
